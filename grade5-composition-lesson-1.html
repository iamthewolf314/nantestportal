Narrative Writing

Facts and Tips

  • A narrative tells a story.
  • Stories are told to inform, entertain and relate an experience.
  • Choose a topic that is familiar to you so you can include clear details.
  • A good narrative usually has a beginning, middle and end.
  • The main parts of a narrative are characters, plot, conflict, setting and point of view.
  • Use transitions such as first, next, then and finally to help your ideas flow.
  • Use describing words and sensory details so the reader can picture what is happening.
  • Think about the mood of the story. Should it feel happy, scary, exciting or peaceful?

Week 2

Narrative Writing: Setting

Facts and Tips

  • Writers choose when and where the story happens.
  • The setting helps the reader imagine the place and time clearly.
  • Describing words such as windy, quiet, muddy and bright make the setting stronger.
  • The tense of the story should match the time of the events.
  • Ask yourself: Was it in the past, is it happening now, or will it happen later?

Week 2

Narrative Writing: Characters

Facts and Tips

  • Characters are the people or animals in a story.
  • Main characters need to be fully developed with plenty of details.
  • Other characters also need enough detail to feel real.
  • Descriptive words help readers imagine each character clearly.

How to describe a character

You can describe a character’s appearance, actions, speech, feelings and behaviour. For example, instead of saying Peter is nice, you can say Peter is a cheerful, energetic boy who always encourages his team-mates.

Practice Example

Characters in a story about an unforgettable experience might include:

Week 3

Descriptive Essay: Planning

Facts and Tips

  • A descriptive essay describes a person, place, object or experience.
  • Think carefully about the topic before writing.
  • Write down the main points you want to develop.
  • Your essay should be written in sequence using paragraphs.
  • Begin every sentence with a capital letter.
  • Use interesting describing words so the reader can picture what you mean.
  • Keep your ideas relevant to the topic.

Week 4

Revising, Editing and Publishing

Facts and Tips

  • Prewrite or plan your ideas before you begin.
  • Write your first draft and get your ideas down.
  • Revise to make the writing better and clearer.
  • Edit to fix grammar, spelling, punctuation and capital letters.
  • Publish by sharing your corrected work with others.
